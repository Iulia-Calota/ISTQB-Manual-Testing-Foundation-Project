2.Static and Dynamic testing:

Static Testing:

Early Phase:

Begins early in the SDLC.

Quality Evaluation:

Evaluates the quality and builds confidence in work products like requirements,code analysis, test plans, test cases, and product backlogs.

Benefits:

Early testing saves time and money, fulfilling the principle of “early testing saves time & money.”

Stakeholder Involvement:

Involves various stakeholders to review and improve the product.

Documentation Review:

Includes reviewing documents such as Software Requirements Specifications (SRS), High-Level Design (HLD), and Low-Level Design (LLD).

Dynamic Testing:

Failure Detection:

Identifies failures and associated defects through subsequent analysis.

Executable Products:

Can only be applied to executable work products.

Functionality Validation:

Validates the functionality of an application.

Types:

Includes Black-box testing and White-box testing.
